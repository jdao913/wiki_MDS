It is notable as a simple example of a fluid flow that can have chaotic trajectories.
It is named after Vladimir Arnold, Eugenio Beltrami, and Steven Childress. Ippolit S. Gromeka's (1881)[3] name has been historically neglected, though much of the discussion has been done by him first.[4]
