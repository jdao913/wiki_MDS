LeSEA Broadcasting is an American Christian television network.[1] Founded by Dr. Lester Sumrall in 1972, LeSEA Broadcasting is headquartered in South Bend, Indiana, and broadcasts Christian and family programming. The late Peter Sumrall, son of Dr. Lester Sumrall, served as its president and chief executive officer from 2002 to 2015.[2] His son, Drew Sumrall, now serves in the same position.[3]

National Channels[edit]
World Harvest Television (WHT)[edit]
World Harvest Television (WHT), channel 367 on DirecTV,[4][5] features some of the world's most popular Christian teachers and leaders, such as Sid Roth, Joseph Prince, Joyce Meyer, and James Robison. WHT is available in over 21.2 million homes across the country on DirecTV, as well as on the Sky Angel Faith Package.
Family Entertainment Television (FETV)[edit]
Family Entertainment Television (FETV), channel 323 on DirecTV,[6][7] channel 82 on Dish Network[8] and channel 578 on AT&T U-Verse,[9] offers viewers a mix of religious and family-friendly programming. FETV provides such programming as Perry Mason,[10] Matlock, the Lone Ranger, Hazel, Bewitched, and Roy Rogers, as well as programs from several popular and well-known ministries, such as Joyce Meyer, James Robison, and Lester Sumrall.
Local Market Stations[edit]
WHMB[edit]
Main article: WHMB-TV
Originally acquired by LeSEA Broadcasting in 1972, WHMB-TV 40 is the longest, continually operated Christian television station in the United States. WHMB reaches the entire Indianapolis, Indiana television market covering over one million households.[11]
WHME[edit]
Main article: WHME-TV
On September 10, 1977, WHME-TV 46 South Bend came onto the airwaves with mostly religious programming, as well as some family programming. Today, WHME is LeSEA Broadcasting's headquarters, housing a number of separate divisions. Its programming is a blend of religion, local sports, and family entertainment.
KWHB[edit]
Main article: KWHB
Purchased by LeSEA Broadcasting in 1985, KWHB-TV 47 is the oldest Christian television station in Tulsa, Oklahoma. Today, KWHB can be viewed on over 84 cable stations in Oklahoma, serving a potential audience of over 1.5 million people.
KWHE[edit]
Main article: KWHE
KWHE TV-14 covers the major population centers of Hilo, Maui, Kona, and Honolulu, with Christian and family programming. The station is carried on Oceanic Cable channel 11 and on Hawaiian Telcom channel 14.
KWHS[edit]
Main article: KWHS-LD
Purchased by LeSEA Broadcasting in 1992, KWHS-LD 51 is the only locally programmed Christian station in Colorado Springs, Colorado.[12]
WHNO[edit]
Main article: WHNO
Acquired by LeSEA in 1994, WHNO TV-20 is New Orleans' only full-power Christian television station.[13]
WCVI[edit]
Main article: WCVI-TV
WCVI-TV 23 was acquired in 2014, and it is the LeSEA station in the U.S. Virgin Islands.
Radio[edit]
Shortwave[edit]
Since 1985 LeSEA Broadcasting has operated World Harvest Radio International, designed to reach the over 200 million shortwave radio receivers in the world. LeSEA is the only Christian broadcaster currently operating a global network of shortwave radio transmitters. Transmitter facilities are located in South Carolina and Palau, Indonesia. Six transmitters are in operation full-time, named the Six Angels.

Angel 1—Covers Africa, North America, Central America, South America, and Australia
Angel 2—Covers parts of North America, Europe, and parts of Asia
Angel 3—Covers Asia
Angel 4—Covers Australia
Angel 5—Covers Africa
Angel 6—Covers North and Central America

Local FM[edit]
Harvest FM[edit]
Harvest 103.1 FM, or WHME (FM), began broadcasting in 1968. The radio station, which serves the South Bend, Indiana area, features a mix of interactive talk, Bible teaching programs, and inspirational music 24 hours a day.
Pulse FM[edit]
Founded in 1996, Pulse FM WHPZ 96.9 broadcasts contemporary Christian music to the Michiana market. In December 2004, LeSEA Broadcasting purchased WDOW 92.1 FM to increase Pulse FM's listening base,[14] and the call letters are now WHPD 92.1. For many years, the radio station hosted World Pulse Festival, a one-day Christian music festival.[15] In 2017, Pulse FM transitioned to a new concert format named Pulse Summer Series, a series of three summer concerts in downtown South Bend, Indiana, at the Morris Performing Arts Center.[16]
